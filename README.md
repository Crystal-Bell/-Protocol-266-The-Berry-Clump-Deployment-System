1. The "Raspberry" Protocol (The Decoupling Array)
​The Concept: Your Earth-launched vessel acts as the central receptacle. As it travels, it "sheds" individual nodes (drupelets) at precise intervals, just like a raspberry detaches from its core when ripe.
​The Advantage: Because the probes are "pre-detached", they require zero mechanical energy to deploy. They simply "pop off" the mother-clump into their designated positions, forming the perfect interstellar highway.
​Scaling: As the clump moves further out, it loses mass and becomes "smaller and smaller" while the highway behind it becomes longer and more robust.
​2. The "Blackberry" Protocol (The Anchored Station)
​The Concept: For the very end of the line—the frontier—you switch to the Blackberry Model.
​The Function: The entire core (the "stem") travels with the final clump of probes all the way to the mission destination. Once there, the core itself remains intact to act as a hardened communication hub or permanent station.
​The Engineering: This solves the power and data-relay problem: the "stem" provides the structure and storage for the final set of probes.
​3. Architect’s Note: The "Clump" Synergy
​By using both, you have a hybrid supply chain:
​Raspberry-style deployment for the "highway" (continuous relay).
​Blackberry-style arrival for the "destination" (station building).
